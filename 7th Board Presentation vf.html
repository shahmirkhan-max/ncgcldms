Impact Assessment Framework

Executive Summary

This framework establishes the methodology to measure, manage, and report on NCGCL's progress against its strategic objectives. It is a critical management tool to ensure our activities create a verifiable developmental impact.

The framework is founded on principles of Mission Alignment, Data-Driven Analysis, and Accountability. It will be managed by a dedicated M&E function, reporting bi-annually to the Board.

Interactive Element: Corporate Logframe

Click on a level of the logical framework to see the corresponding metrics and targets.

Level 1: Inputs (Our Resources)

The capital, people, and systems we deploy.

  • Financial Capital: Total equity and borrowing capacity available.
  • Human Capital: Staff deployed, PFI training sessions held.
  • Systems: Digital guarantee platform deployed and operational.

Level 2: Outputs (Direct Results)

The direct products of our activities.

  • Guarantees Issued (PKR): Volume of guarantees provided to PFIs.
  • Guarantees Issued (#): Number of individual SMEs/loans supported.
  • PFIs Partnered: Number of active financial institutions in the program.
  • Thematic Focus: % of portfolio to Climate, Inclusion, or regional targets.

Level 3: Outcomes (Changes in Behavior)

The changes our outputs create in our partners and end-borrowers.

  • For PFIs: Increased % of SME lending in their total portfolio (additionality).
  • For PFIs: Reduced collateral requirements or improved pricing for guaranteed loans.
  • For SMEs: % of firms reporting access to finance as "less of a barrier."
  • For SMEs: % of firms accessing formal credit for the first time ("new to bank").

Level 4: Impact (Long-Term Strategic Goals)

The ultimate contribution to NCGCL's mission.

  • Job Creation: Net new jobs created by supported SMEs.
  • Revenue Growth: % increase in annual sales for supported SMEs.
  • Formalization: % of firms moving from informal to formal sector.
  • Climate: CO2 emissions reduced or avoided from green finance portfolio.

Decision Required

The Board is requested to review and approve the NCGCL Impact Assessment Framework as the standard for measuring and reporting developmental performance.

Agenda 7: Tax Optimization & Borrowing

Executive Summary

This proposal aims to optimize NCGCL's effective tax rate and ROE. The strategy has two parts:

  • Tax-Efficient Investments: A target allocation of 50% in Banking Placements, 25% in MTBs (GOP T-Bills), and 25% in High-Grade Commercial Paper.
  • Leverage: Establishing borrowing lines (modelled at PKR 2.0B, with authority requested up to PKR 4.0B), secured against GoP securities. The interest expense creates a tax shield, reducing taxable income.

Based on the 12-month projections from the new workbook, the full strategy is expected to reduce the effective tax rate from ~35.2% to ~32.8%, generating an incremental PAT of ~PKR 49 million.

Agenda 8: Credit Risk & Capital Management Policy

Executive Summary

This policy establishes the framework for managing credit risk and ensuring capital adequacy. It introduces an internal model for calculating Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR), moving beyond simple leverage.

Key pillars include:

  • Capital Model: Defines Core Capital, Risk Capital, and Tier 2 Capital to calculate total capital available.
  • Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA): Calculated internally based on portfolio risk, not just nominal value.
  • CAR Target: A minimum CAR of 20% is required (vs. 10% regulatory), with a target of 25%.
  • Risk-Based Pricing: A matrix to determine guarantee fees based on Probability of Default (PD) and Loss Given Default (LGD).
  • Capital Intensity Model: A formula to adjust required capital based on transaction structure (e.g., first-loss vs. second-loss).

Agenda 9: Operations Manual

Executive Summary

This Operations Manual is presented for Board approval as the comprehensive guide for all NCGCL operational activities. It standardizes procedures and defines responsibilities across the entire guarantee lifecycle to ensure efficiency, risk management, and compliance.

The manual covers 10 key sections, from PFI Onboarding and Guarantee Origination to Claims Management and Data Security. It is designed to be a living document, reviewed every two years, and will be digitized to support workflows.

Interactive Element: The Guarantee Lifecycle

Click through the phases to see the standardized process flow as defined in the manual.

Phase 1: PFI Onboarding (Section 3)

  • Application: PFI submits standardized application and due diligence pack.
  • Due Diligence: NCGCL (Risk & Compliance) assesses PFI's financial health, risk management, AML/CFT controls, and operational capacity.
  • Approval: Recommendation presented to the Guarantee Committee (GC) and/or Board for approval.
  • Agreement: Legal executes the Master Credit Guarantee Agreement (MCGA) upon approval.
  • System Setup: PFI is configured in the NCGCL digital guarantee platform.

Phase 2: Guarantee Origination (Section 4)

  • Submission: PFI submits borrower/facility details via the digital platform, confirming adherence to the agreed Guarantee Policy.
  • Screening: NCGCL system performs automated checks (eligibility, AML, limits).
  • Risk Assessment: NCGCL Risk team runs internal scoring, assigns risk rating, and confirms pricing.
  • Approval: Approved/rejected based on delegated authority matrix (GC or Management).
  • Issuance: System issues digital guarantee certificate; guarantee fee is invoiced.

Phase 3: Portfolio Monitoring (Section 6)

  • PFI Reporting: PFIs submit mandatory monthly/quarterly reports on portfolio performance (e.g., repayments, NPLs, risk ratings).
  • NCGCL Analysis: Risk team aggregates data, monitors covenant compliance, and tracks portfolio concentrations (sector, PFI, geography).
  • Watchlist: Facilities classified as "Substandard" or "Doubtful" are placed on an internal watchlist for heightened review.
  • Reporting: Quarterly portfolio health reports are submitted to the GC and the Board.

Phase 4: Claims & Recovery (Section 7)

  • Loss Recognition: PFI notifies NCGCL of a default event (e.g., 90+ DPD) as per MCGA.
  • Claim Submission: PFI submits a formal claim using the standardized template after the "waiting period" (e.g., 180 DPD).
  • Validation: NCGCL Claims unit validates the claim against the guarantee terms (eligibility, documentation, PFI compliance).
  • Payout: Approved claims are paid out within the specified period (e.g., 30 days).
  • Recovery: NCGCL and PFI commence recovery actions. All recovered funds are shared proportionally as defined in the MCGA.

The Board is requested to review and approve the NCGCL Operations Manual as the standard for all company operations.

Agenda 11: AML/CFT Policy

Executive Summary

This policy establishes NCGCL's framework to mitigate risks of Money Laundering (ML) and Terrorist Financing (TF). It is based on a "structured reliance" model, acknowledging that Partner Financial Institutions (PFIs) conduct the primary KYC/CDD on end-borrowers.

NCGCL's controls are therefore focused on:

  • PFI Due Diligence: Robust onboarding and continuous monitoring of PFIs' own AML/CFT programs.
  • Data-Sharing: Mandating PFIs to provide end-borrower data via digital channels for screening.
  • Independent Screening: NCGCL performs its own automated, real-time screening of all parties (borrowers, directors) against sanctions lists (UN, NACTA, etc.).
  • Governance: Appointing a Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) and establishing clear escalation paths for high-risk clients or matches.

Interactive Element: Risk-Based Approach in Action

This demonstrates how the policy is applied. Select a scenario to see NCGCL's risk-based controls.

Controls: Low Risk (Standard SME)

  • PFI Action: PFI conducts Standard Customer Due Diligence (CDD).
  • NCGCL Action 1: Rely on PFI's CDD (as per Annex A).
  • NCGCL Action 2: PFI submits borrower data (name, ID#) to NCGCL portal.
  • NCGCL Action 3: Automated system screens data against UN/NACTA lists. (Result: No Match)
  • Outcome: Guarantee application proceeds automatically.

Controls: Medium Risk (Politically Exposed Person - PEP)

  • PFI Action: PFI conducts Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) and gets senior management approval.
  • NCGCL Action 1: PFI submits borrower data to NCGCL portal; flags as PEP.
  • NCGCL Action 2: Automated system screens and confirms PEP status. (Result: PEP Match)
  • NCGCL Action 3: Application is flagged and routed to NCGCL's Chief Compliance Officer (CCO).
  • NCGCL Action 4: CCO reviews PFI's EDD, assesses the risk, and provides approval/rejection.
  • Outcome: Guarantee requires manual CCO approval.

Controls: High Risk (Sanctions Match)

  • PFI Action: PFI's initial screening identifies a potential sanctions match and blocks the transaction.
  • NCGCL Action 1: PFI submits data to NCGCL portal.
  • NCGCL Action 2: Automated system screens data. (Result: Positive Match on Sanctions List)
  • NCGCL Action 3: Application is AUTOMATICALLY REJECTED.
  • NCGCL Action 4: Alert is escalated to CCO and Management.
  • Outcome: Guarantee is prohibited. NCGCL coordinates with PFI on any required regulatory reporting (e.g., to FMU).

The Board is requested to review and approve the NCGCL AML/CFT Policy.

Board Evaluation Framework

Executive Summary & Philosophy

This framework establishes a formal, annual process to assess and enhance the effectiveness of the Board, its Committees, and individual Directors. The philosophy is not one of compliance, but rather to use evaluation as a vital strategic instrument for continuous improvement, critical self-reflection, and accountability.

The process is led by the Human Resources & Nomination Committee (HRNC) and confidentially administered by the Company Secretary. An independent external facilitator will be engaged at least once every three years.

1. Collective Board Evaluation

Focuses on the Board's performance as a unit.

  • Strategic Alignment & Oversight
  • Risk Governance & Internal Control
  • Monitoring of Management & CEO
  • Board Dynamics, Culture & Ethics
  • Stakeholder & Regulatory Relations

2. Committee & Individual Evaluation

Assesses the specific effectiveness of committees and directors.

  • Committees: Fulfillment of Terms of Reference (TORs), efficiency, and quality of reporting to the Board.
  • Individual Directors: Preparation, participation, strategic contribution, independence, and relevant skills.
  • Peer Evaluation: Provides 360-degree feedback on individual director contributions.

Interactive Element: The Annual Evaluation Cycle

Click through the phases to see the evaluation process from start to finish. The primary output is a formal Action Plan.

Phase 1: Data Collection (Annual)

  • Administrator: Company Secretary, under HRNC oversight.
  • Tools:
    • Confidential self-assessment surveys (for Board & Committees).
    • Confidential peer-assessment surveys (for Individual Directors).
    • Objective data (e.g., meeting attendance, skill matrix).
  • External Facilitation: Engaged at least once every 3 years to ensure objectivity.

Phase 2: Aggregation, Analysis & Reporting

  • Aggregation: Company Secretary compiles and anonymizes all survey responses and objective data into a single, aggregated report.
  • Review: The HRNC reviews the aggregated report first. They analyze key themes, strengths, and areas for development.
  • Report Finalization: The HRNC finalizes the report and its key findings to be presented to the full Board.

Phase 3: Board Discussion & Feedback

  • Presentation: The Chair of the HRNC presents the aggregated report and findings to the full Board in a dedicated session.
  • Facilitated Discussion: The Board engages in an open, constructive discussion on the results, moderated by the Chairman (or external facilitator).
  • Individual Feedback:
    • The Chairman provides private feedback to each Director.
    • The HRNC Chair provides private feedback to the Chairman.

Phase 4: Action Plan & Follow-up (The "Way Forward")

  • Formal Plan: Based on the discussion, a formal Board Evaluation Action Plan is developed.
  • Content: The plan identifies specific, measurable, and time-bound actions to address identified gaps or enhancement areas.
  • Linkages: The plan directly informs:
    • Board & Committee succession planning.
    • Individual Director training and development plans.
    • Improvements to governance processes or Committee TORs.
  • Monitoring: The HRNC is responsible for monitoring the implementation of the Action Plan throughout the year.

Way Forward & Decision Required

"The Board is requested to review and formally approve the Board Evaluation Framework and Policy."

"Upon approval, the Board authorizes the HRNC and the Company Secretary to initiate the first evaluation cycle for the current fiscal year, with findings and a draft action plan to be presented to the Board in accordance with the outlined timeline."
